This work investigated the impact of enabling multi-link P-EDCA contention in a STA MLD. In particular, it compared behavior before and after allowing P-EDCA to contend across multiple links, with the objective of understanding whether multi-link P-EDCA improves overall system behavior in terms of throughput, fairness, delay, and contention efficiency.

**Thesis Conclusion**

This thesis investigated the effect of enabling P-EDCA on one link versus both links in multi-link Wi-Fi scenarios under mixed access-category traffic with strong VO prominence. The results show that the choice of P-EDCA link policy materially affects throughput, fairness, delay tails, and internal P-EDCA behavior. A single-link P-EDCA policy frequently reduced P-EDCA failures and resets, indicating a more stable and efficient escalation process. In several mild or moderately stressed cases, single-link P-EDCA also improved P-EDCA VO throughput or delay behavior. However, in higher-contention or higher P-EDCA-share scenarios, enabling P-EDCA on both links often produced a stronger raw P-EDCA throughput advantage. The study therefore shows that no single static policy is universally optimal. Instead, the effectiveness of one-link or both-link P-EDCA depends on the offered load, contention level, and the relative distribution of P-EDCA and EDCA clients. The main conclusion is that P-EDCA link enablement should be adaptive rather than fixed.

**Future Work**

A natural next step is to design an adaptive P-EDCA controller that dynamically selects one-link or both-link enablement based on observed network state. Such a controller could use metrics such as contention level, queue occupancy, recent P-EDCA failures and resets, delay tails, and the proportion of P-EDCA to EDCA clients. Future work should also evaluate more advanced link-selection policies beyond fixed or round-robin assignment, including contention-aware and load-aware preferred-link selection. In addition, the present work could be extended by testing a wider range of traffic mixes, mobility conditions, PHY settings, and longer simulation durations, as well as by averaging across multiple seeds to improve statistical confidence. Finally, separating and analyzing per-AC and per-link behavior in greater detail would help refine the design of adaptive multi-link P-EDCA for practical deployments.
